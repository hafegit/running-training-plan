🏁 Race 1 Strategy — 3-Leg Road Half (May 29)

Format: 3-leg relay-style half marathon on road. Each leg is roughly 4.4 miles.

Pacing Strategy — Negative Split:

  • Leg 1 (Miles 1-4.4): 9:30-9:45/mi — RPE 6-7. Hold back! Bank energy, not time. Settle into rhythm.
  • Leg 2 (Miles 4.4-8.8): 9:15-9:30/mi — RPE 7-8. Gradually increase effort. Stay smooth.
  • Leg 3 (Miles 8.8-13.1): 9:00-9:15/mi — RPE 8-9. Leave it all out there. This is where training pays off.

Goal Time: Sub-2:02 (avg ~9:18/mi)

Fueling: Gel at mile 5 and mile 9. Sip water at every aid station.

Warm-up: 10 min easy jog + hip prehab + 3 strides

⛰️ Race 2 Strategy — Trail Half (Jun 27)

Format: Trail half marathon with significant elevation gain at altitude.

Pacing Strategy — Effort-Based:

  • Uphills: Power hike ALL of them from mile 1. Arms pumping, short steps. RPE 7-8. Do NOT try to run steep uphills.
  • Flats: Run at 10:00-10:30/mi. Smooth and controlled.
  • Downhills: Quick cadence, soft landings, slight forward lean. Don't brake — let gravity help.

Goal: Finish strong and enjoy the mountains!

Fueling: Eat every 30-45 min (gels, chews, or real food). Sip water constantly — altitude dehydrates faster.

Altitude Tips: Slow down 10-15% from sea-level pace. If you feel dizzy/nauseous, walk and breathe deeply. Hydrate aggressively starting 2 days before.

🏔️ Altitude Tips

  • Expect to be 10-15% slower than sea level — this is normal, not a fitness issue
  • Hydrate aggressively: aim for clear/light yellow urine starting 48 hours before
  • Reduce caffeine and alcohol 2 days before race
  • If you feel dizzy, nauseous, or get a headache: slow down, breathe deeply, walk
  • Arrive at altitude as early as possible to start acclimatizing
  • Sleep with head elevated if you feel short of breath at night
  • Eat easily digestible carbs — altitude can suppress appetite

🚦 Workout Priority System

Each workout has a priority level that determines what happens if you miss it:

  • 🔴 Critical — Long runs, race-day taper runs. MUST reschedule if missed. These build the endurance foundation.
  • 🟡 Important — Tempo runs, hill repeats, speed work. Try to reschedule within 2 days. If you can't, skip it — don't compress the schedule.
  • 🟢 Flexible — Easy runs, rest days. Auto-skip if missed. These support recovery but aren't make-or-break.
  • ⚪ Lifting — Strength sessions. Skip if missed — never compress by doubling up. Consistency matters more than any single session.

Golden Rules:

  • Never stack two hard efforts back-to-back
  • Keep at least 1 rest day per week
  • Never push workouts past race-week boundary
  • If 3+ consecutive days missed: consider a recovery week
