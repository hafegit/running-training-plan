## 🏁 Race Day Goals & Strategy

### Race 1: Reno-Tahoe Odyssey (May 29)
- **Goal time:** Sub-2:01 (~9:12/mi average)
- **Strategy:** Negative split across the 3 legs
  - Leg 1 (Miles 1-4.4): 9:15-9:30/mi — RPE 6-7. Hold back!
  - Leg 2 (Miles 4.4-8.8): 9:00-9:15/mi — RPE 7-8. Build effort.
  - Leg 3 (Miles 8.8-13.1): 8:45-9:00/mi — RPE 8-9. Leave it all out there.
- Use breaks between legs to hydrate, splash water, take 3 deep breaths, reset mentally.

### Race 2: Broken Arrow Skyrace (Jun 27)
- **Goal:** Finish strong — effort-based, not pace-based
- **Strategy:**
  - Power hike ALL steep uphills from mile 1 — this is strategy, not weakness
  - Run flats at 10:00-10:30/mi
  - Quick cadence on downhills, soft landings
  - Eat every 30-45 min, sip water every 15 min
  - Slow down 10-15% from what feels right at altitude
  - Layers! UV protection! More fuel than you think!

## 📋 Workout Priority Guide

| Priority | Meaning | If Missed... |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| 🔴 Critical | Long runs, race-week taper runs | Must reschedule to next available day |
| 🟡 Key Workout | Tempo, hills, speed intervals | Try to reschedule within 2 days, otherwise skip |
| 🟣 Lift | Weight training | Skip — don't compress lifting days |
| 🟢 Flexible | Easy runs, cross-training, rest | Skip and move on |

## ⚠️ Rescheduling Rules

1. Never stack two hard efforts back-to-back
2. Always preserve at least 1 rest day per week
3. Never push a workout past its race-week boundary
4. If 3+ days missed in a row, treat it as a down week and resume next week

## 🏔️ Altitude Tips

- Training at 5,000 ft, racing at 6,000-8,000 ft
- Expect 10-15% performance decrease at race altitude
- Hydrate aggressively — 80-100oz water daily in race weeks
- Add electrolytes (Nuun, LMNT, or pinch of salt + lemon)
- Arrive at altitude as early as possible before race day
- If you feel dizzy, nauseous, or have a headache — slow down immediately
